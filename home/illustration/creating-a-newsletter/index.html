I was asked to create a newsletter for a partner of my organisation. They were a youth group and so I incorporated their ideas to allow them to authentically represent their group.

Recently the YCAs started creating a newsletter. They wrote up the copy and chose pictures for the newsletter that described their actions over the past few months. However the copy did not yet have a design and a layout

I was asked to add design features to help make their newsletter a little more trendy.

#1 Brief

After speaking to someone involved in the project I was informed as to what was desired for the project. Here was my interpretation of the brief:

The newsletter should match the brand identity of the YCA’s;

  • Bright, colourful
  • Professional approach
  • Hard workers and confident
  • Young, speaking for the youth.

Example of the copy:

The copy had several sections: An introduction, Our logo journey, Meeting Kersten England, Mask competition, Behind the mask and Outreach sessions.

#2 Target market segment

This newsletter should be something that the youth group can use as a tool to when doing their work. It should be something they should be able to:

  • Share with young people to explain who they to help get their message across and
  • Share with adults to explain who they are to encourage them to support their actions.
  • Share with organisations to show the merit of their actions and allow them to access extra resources.
  • A record for the future for the youth group to look back on.

#3 Market research

To create a good newsletter I decided to look for inspiration from some professional sources of modern magazine design:

From here I was able to understand my taste and what I was interested in conveying within my own newsletter:

  1. How typography can play on the cover with the images on the front
  2. A good consistent style that tied the pages together.
  3. Good use of colour and style that wouldn’t overpower the text within the newsletter.
  4. and also large blocks of text can be fashioned within the newsletter into something

#4 Drafts or selection process

To stay honest to the original intent of the YCA’s I set my self these requirements:

  1. Make minimal changes to the copy.
  2. Use all of their images provided.
  3. Not change their message or the tone of their work.

Considerations:

  • Fonts for this project: I decided to use Atkinson hyperlegible. This is a professional font made by the brailleinstitute.org to be an incredibly easy to read font and increase the potential reach of the newsletter. This font has a professional feel that suited the newsletter.
  • Strong colours: The team love colour so the newsletter had to be bright and fun.

#5 Creating the assets

Beginning with the front cover the first thing I decided to do was to work out how to make use of the first photo. The first photo was a landscape photo of students studying. After looking at the reference images I felt that by making this photo into a shape I could find some interesting ways to incorporate text and colour into it.

I cropped with image out using affinity photo and then moved it over into affinity designer.

With this draft created I started changing elements. My first decision was that I needed a good colour scheme for the newsletter. as this pale blue wasn’t working.

To achieve this I looked at a colour scheme making website and imported the colour scheme for the logo I had made into it

I got this image when I put my colours into the scheme maker and very quickly I was impressed by the aesthetic of the large bands of colour.

I decided to use this as the whole design for the newsletter.

With this colourful background I decided to try incorporate the image back into the page. This was done by making it black and white and then setting the layer to multiply so it would overlay over the colour underneath it.

#6 Putting the media together

Once I had created the front cover and got positive feedback on it I took design elements from this and incorporated them into the future pages.

To do this I followed a simple formula, the methodology of this is similar to bin packing or gant charts in mathematics. The most important elements that will be the largest on the page are incorporated into the design first as they need the most space so its important to get them into place before other elements of the design take up all of the space that they would need.

  1. Add the borders, page number and colour bar
  2. Add the title
  3. Add the images
  4. Add the text
  5. Add boxes to break up the page and move some of the text around.

With the design I incorporated ideas from student newsletter, bauhaus design and art magazines. Using affinity designer I was able to see all of the pages together using art boards which made it very easy to get a unified design feel throughout the project.

#7 Feedback

The team were very positive about the colour schemes. We spent some time improving the layouts of the text on the pages to make them read easier.

#8 Final version

The final version underwent multiple rounds of editing to improve the copy, layout and format.

#9 Implementation in campaign

This newsletter was well received by the team. They printed it out and the volunteers handed these out as part of their work to young people.

With this created the team asked for a second issue to be made which incorporated a new set of colours in this case Bradford Bulls as they had been nice enough to work with the youth group for this issue.

I started to think about different design features I wanted to be incorporated into this newsletter. The faded out images. The bands of colour. Overlapping textures.

The Ben Day dot was a new feature for this newsletter that I made by editing the images in photoshop.

Tree cover

For the last one newsletter I was sent a video that the group had put together as inspiration for the front cover of the newsletter. I found this a good starting inspiration and so began working from there.

#1 Brief

After speaking to the client they explained the videos message to me.

The idea was that its always impressed upon young people that everything has to be done now or they will ruin their futures and over Covid the inability to do things has left a lot of them very stressed.

This video uses a sign post with aphorism’s to hurry up as a metaphor that stress and trying to get things done at the moment is a path that leads nowhere.

The main ideas to express were:

  1. A path through the forest.
  2. Make it appeal to young people
  3. A signpost with confusing directions.

#2 Collecting references

With a project like this working from life is important to push a work beyond representational to relatable. With this in mind I went out and took some photos in my local area as inspiration.

I also collected some extra references from the internet that would get the feeling of a tree lined path that I was after.

#3 Drawing

With these references at hand I began by creating a drawing.

Drawn on A3 paper, I began by sketching out shapes of trees following the vanishing point of the path. I then added some simple wood textures with some areas of shading.

I was interested in using the curves of the trees to frame the path and by having strong curves accentuate the path and make the vanishing point more evident by having a dramatic difference between the foreground, midground and background scales.

Once scanned I made a few changes to make it read better. First I altered the levels to get a strong contrast between the paper and the pencil. I also used the blemish tool to remove a hair that had been scanned and then painted over a colour overlay of an area that had scanned slightly dark on the page.

#4 Digital work.

Blocking out the sections

I scanned and moved the project over to Affinity designer. There I began by creating curves over each of the elements to separate them out. I had a layer for the sun, sky, sea, path, trees, signposts and foliage.

This would make it easy to recolour the work quickly and work upon them separately.

Shading

From this I added a second layer under each that I started creating shadows for. This was done by following two principles.

  1. Things facing the light will be brighter.
  2. Things close to each other will occlude each other.

I created a palette moving from highlights to shadow. I formed curves within each of the layers for each of these then added the shadow within that.

Colour

With this completed I then set a copy of the section layers above to colour blend mode. This would tint the layers below and allowed me to get some quick easy colour work done that preserved the shading and drawing beneath.

One I was happy with the colours I then added some gradient blends to these colour layers to add some extra interest moving from light to dark, and adding some purples and pinks.

To express the feeling of being inside of a forest more I created 2 layers the first was a circle set to vivid light a warm yellow that I put over the sun to make it look like its radiating warmth. Then I added a vignette that was transparent in the middle set to multiply that added extra colour to the edges of the forest.

Finally I added the title and footer to the image and sent it to my client for feedback.
